I know that it’ll take a lot of practice to get used to all the selectors and options available in CSS but I’m excited to get into it. I love how the reading, lecture, as well as the video, were all related in some way. I particularly enjoyed the video about bad doors (It’s not you. Bad doors are everywhere 2016) as it explored the importance of design decisions and the general interface and interaction with things. Don Norman speaks about how discoverability and feedback are important in the design of things. This really struck a cord with me and it’s something that I will continue to think about whenever I make something. Don says, “why do you have to have a sign that says push or pull? Why not make it obvious?”. This point ties into one from the reading, “traditional conventions are repetitions that become regularities against which differences stand out” (Mailloux 2015: 400). When practices are repeated it becomes a convention that is understood by the majority of people. For example, the colour red has always been made to signify danger. Through the repeated use of this colour to represent danger, it is now a convention for representing danger, and just like Don said, there is now no need to point out what it means, it is obvious. This can also tie in with web design since there are already certain conventions and repeated practices which make it easier for people to understand. If I place a button or link on my website, having it highlighted when hovered over or when clicked on is a repeated practice and a convention for good usability and a good interface. It makes the action obvious and it gives the user feedback. Since web design is all about the user interface and experience, it is important to know how to design an interface that will give off a good experience. Watching the past lecture gave me abit of a flashback to our first year of uni and having all our lectures online, just seeing our lecturers faces on a small floating bubble. I do feel slightly overwhelmed when consuming multiple sources of information as I get accustomed to a particular person and particular teaching method, so I sometimes find it difficult to adjust to another as well. However, the past lecture was helpful as it dove into additional information, such as the use of ‘before’ and ‘after’ selectors. There are so many things to learn within all these coding languages, and the more you interact with it and the more content you absorb the more well versed you will become with it.

Week 4 Blog

This week was slightly overwhelming, as we already moved on to JavaScript, whilst I’m still trying to learn HTML and CSS. Three different languages over 3 weeks scares me, however, it was super exciting to learn about the basics of Javascript. Seeing where websites gets its functionality from and the insane things you can do with it is quite cool. I really enjoyed this weeks readings, especially the Basic Principles of User Interface Design one, (Studio by UXPin, 2020). From the 14 principles they mentioned, I began to take note of how many of those principles have I implemented and how many have I not even thought of. For example, I was planning on making a minimalistic and simple website, which ties in with the 4th principle on aiming for simplicity. When looking at the wireframes I’ve made, I immediately noticed what needed to be changed. For my blog page wireframe, I did not think about making the blogs easily accessible, I just assumed that the viewers would scroll down, but now I realize that I should add in hyperlinks to take the viewers to each blog post. We described interactivity as something that is responsive, requires effort, and does not respond in a random way. Therefore, UI directly relates to interactivity. UI is what allows users to respond to something and receive feedback, and it requires users to put in effort to use it. UI is essentially the skeleton for interactivity on websites and therefore it shows just how important good UI and UX design is.

Reference: Studio by UXPin (2020). The Basic Principles of User Interface Design. [online] Studio by UXPin. Available at: https://www.uxpin.com/studio/blog/ui-design-principles/.

In terms of the IxD process, the first step is the goal alignment. The goal of my website is to represent who I am and to inform viewers on who I am and what it is that I do. For now the target audience is just peers, tutors and lecturers, but for the future the target audience would be companies that are looking to potentially hire me. In order for me to reach this goal I need to think about how I’d like the website to represent me and what exactly it is that I’d like to showcase. I have already done my content mapping with my wireframes and I have an overall idea of what I want to put where. I haven’t yet done a user flow diagram and it’s definitely something that I should do so that I can see whether my UI design is efficient or not. In terms of interface elements, the only thing I have and I felt that I needed for my website so far is buttons and text with hyperlinks.
